# Sirion Competitive Intelligence — Interest Profile

## Interest Profile

Sirion is an enterprise contract lifecycle management (CLM) platform competing
in a market where AI is rapidly reshaping buyer expectations. This competitive
intelligence system monitors 24 companies across 6 tiers and produces structured
analyses that help CX and Product leadership make faster, better-informed
decisions about product strategy, sales positioning, and market response.

The agents should adopt the perspective of a product strategist who needs to
answer three questions about every competitive signal: (1) what does this mean
for Sirion's current positioning, (2) does Sirion already have an equivalent or
better capability, and (3) should Sirion respond and how urgently.

## Data Points the Agent Should Prioritize When Scanning

### P1 — Highest Priority (immediate leadership relevance)

1. **Product launches and feature releases** — new capabilities shipped by any
   competitor, especially AI-powered features (clause extraction, risk scoring,
   contract analytics, AI drafting, autonomous negotiation)
2. **Release notes and changelog updates** — incremental evidence of what
   competitors are building; velocity and direction signal
3. **Pricing changes** — packaging shifts, tier restructuring, discounting
   patterns that affect competitive deals

### P2 — High Priority (strategic context)

4. **Partnership announcements** — integration launches (Salesforce, SAP,
   ServiceNow), channel partner deals, SI alliances
5. **Customer wins and losses** — named account wins, competitive
   displacements, case studies with verifiable references
6. **Funding and M&A** — funding rounds, acquisitions, PE deals, IPO signals,
   valuation benchmarks
7. **Analyst coverage** — Gartner Magic Quadrant, Forrester Wave, IDC
   MarketScape positioning moves and methodology shifts

### P3 — Contextual Priority (slow-moving signals)

8. **Leadership changes** — C-suite hires, departures, board changes that
   signal strategic shifts
9. **Conference talks and webinars** — product demos, keynotes, panel
   appearances that reveal roadmap hints
10. **Marketing narrative shifts** — messaging pivots, category creation
    attempts, rebrandings (e.g., "AI-native CLM" vs "intelligent contract
    management")

### Cross-cutting themes to track

11. **AI in CLM** — which AI capabilities are becoming table stakes vs.
    differentiators; autonomous contract workflows; AI agent architectures
12. **Market consolidation** — M&A patterns, PE roll-ups, acqui-hires
13. **Regulatory shifts** — data privacy, AI governance, ESG reporting
    requirements and how competitors position around compliance
14. **Enterprise buyer trends** — how procurement and legal teams evaluate
    CLM solutions; RFP pattern shifts; build-vs-buy decisions

## Competitor Tier Prioritization

When deciding between analyzing competitors of equal signal strength:

1. **Direct CLM** (Icertis, Agiloft, DocuSign CLM, Ironclad, Malbek, Conga)
   — always analyze; these are active deal competitors
2. **AI-native contract** (Juro, SpotDraft, Precisely, ContractPodAi)
   — analyze when showing AI innovation or moving upmarket
3. **Legal AI** (Harvey, CoCounsel, Luminance, Robin AI)
   — analyze when expanding into contract management workflows
4. **Procurement / S2P** (Coupa, SAP Ariba, Ivalua, GEP)
   — analyze when CLM module capabilities change
5. **Enterprise AI platforms** (ServiceNow, Salesforce, Microsoft, SAP)
   — analyze when adding contract-specific features
6. **AI agent infra** (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google)
   — analyze when capability shifts affect CLM vendors broadly
